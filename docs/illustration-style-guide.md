# Dance On Time — Illustration Style Guide

Derived by close study of the three canonical illustrations — the only reference that matters:

- `images/illustrations/country-swing.png` — energetic country two-step couple
- `images/illustrations/bride-groom.png` — tender first-dance couple on a checker floor
- `images/illustrations/dance-party.png` — full reception: bride & groom, 8 guests, a DJ

Everything below is observed from these. New artwork must look like it belongs in this set.
(Note: these are hand-inked/painted **raster** illustrations. In-repo hand-built SVG can only
approximate them — for true matches, generate raster art from the prompt in §11 and treat this
guide as the art direction.)

---

## 1. One-line summary
Clean hand-inked line art of wholesome retro-Americana social dancers, **black line + white +
soft grays with a single red (#e5261f) accent**, natural adult proportions, lots of motion, on a
white "room" backdrop with faint gray props.

## 2. Color — the whole palette
- **Line:** warm near-black (~`#1a1a1a`). Not pure black.
- **White:** background, skin, shirts, wedding gown. **Skin is white with an outline — never a
  flesh tone.**
- **Grays (this is important — the set is mostly grayscale):** a light-to-mid warm gray does most
  of the work — men's suits and trousers, the cowboy hat, DJ gear and speakers, and all the soft
  fold-shading. Roughly: fill `#c9c9cc`, shadow `#9a9a9d`, plus lighter `#e8e8e8` for the faintest
  props. Multiple gentle gray steps, not one flat gray.
- **Red `#e5261f` — the ONLY saturated color.** It carries: the ONE red dress, the heroine/bride's
  hair, the groom's bowtie + boutonnière, a guest's necktie, and the red squares of the checker
  floor. In dress folds the red deepens slightly (~`#b81c17`). Nothing else is ever colored.
- **ONE red dress per image (rule, 2026-07):** only a SINGLE person — the hero/lead woman — wears an
  all-red dress. Other women wear white/light-gray/black (with at most a tiny red accent), NOT red.
  Small red accents (a tie, belt, flower) on a few figures are fine; multiple full red dresses are
  not, unless explicitly requested. (Supersedes the older reference art where several guest women are
  in red — e.g. `dance-party.png`.)
- **Black:** hair (men and guest women), shoes, boots, belts, the DJ's cap, mixer gear.

**Discipline:** if you can name a second saturated hue anywhere, it's wrong. No blue/green/
yellow/brown, no gradients-as-style, no neon.

## 3. Line quality
- Confident, smooth, slightly **tapered** strokes (calligraphic — thicker on outer contours,
  thinner on interior detail like folds, facial features, stitching). *This weight variation is
  what my flat, uniform-width SVG strokes were missing.*
- Rounded, closed joints. No sketchy multi-strokes, no visible construction lines.
- `country-swing.png` is a touch more painterly/brushy; `bride-groom` and `dance-party` are a
  little cleaner/thinner. Both are acceptable within the set — aim between them.

## 4. Shading
- Soft, minimal, **gray tonal** — light gray fills dropped into fold/shadow areas (behind the
  knee, under a lapel, gown volume, shirt creases). Looks like light marker/wash, a couple of
  steps, never cross-hatching, never hard cel edges.
- The red dress gets a slightly darker-red in its skirt folds.
- Every figure sits on a **soft light-gray floor shadow** (a loose ellipse, no hard edge).

## 5. Characters — proportions & faces
- **Natural adults, ~7–7.5 heads tall.** Believable, lightly stylized — NOT chibi/big-head, and
  not the stubby blobs my SVG produced.
- **Cast variety = subtle (rule, 2026-07).** Everyone has the **same neutral white skin** (outline
  only, no flesh tones, no dark skin) and reads as an adult **~25–65** (no children, no elderly).
  Make figures look like different individuals through **hairstyle, hair colour (black/grey), outfit
  cut, build/height and dance style** — never through skin tone or extreme age. Keep a roughly
  **~50/50 mix of men and women** across the cast (never all one gender).
- **Couples only, never singles (rule, 2026-07).** Every dancer is **partnered** in a man-and-woman
  couple — no solo or unpartnered dancer anywhere, including the background, so the dancer count is
  always **even**. Each couple sits in a clean **connected** frame where joined hands actually meet —
  no tangled/floating hands. (Non-dancing figures such as a DJ are not subject to this.)
- Almost always drawn in **¾ profile facing their partner.**
- **Face kit (consistent across all figures):** simple curved eyebrows; small almond eyes (a
  curve + a dot, a hint of lashes on women); a small nose (a soft bump in profile); a small
  closed smile; and a few **freckle dots on the cheek** — the freckles are a signature, don't
  omit them. Ears simple. Expressions: warm, happy, focused on the partner.
- **Hands are articulated** — real fingers, wrapping in a handhold or forming a fist/point. Not
  mittens.
- **Feet wear real footwear** — cowboy boots, heels, dress shoes — and are planted or mid-step
  with visible weight.

## 6. Wardrobe rules (who wears what)
- **Men:** BLACK hair, neat (side-part quiff, sideburns). Grayscale wardrobe — either a light-gray
  notch-lapel suit, or a white/light dress shirt (often sleeves rolled) with gray trousers, black
  belt, black shoes. Their one allowed color hit is a **red tie/bowtie**. Groom adds a red
  boutonnière.
- **Women:** the **red dress** is the signature (halter, off-shoulder, cap-sleeve, wrap — always
  knee-length and flaring with movement; black heels or black cowboy boots), but **only ONE woman
  per image wears it** — the hero/lead. Hair: **red for the heroine/bride** (loose waves, or a low
  bun with the wedding veil); **black for other women.** Additional/guest women wear white, light-gray
  or black dresses (at most a tiny red accent), NOT red. So per image: exactly one red dress + red
  hair on the lead; everyone else grayscale.
- **Western storytelling details** (country-swing): pearl-snap shirt with a pointed **yoke** and
  two flap chest pockets, rolled sleeves, big oval **belt buckle**, gray jeans with a center
  crease, a **light-gray cowboy hat** with a darker band, **black cowboy boots with white
  scroll/wing stitching**, and a **red polka-dot circle skirt with a white petticoat ruffle** at
  the hem.
- **Formal storytelling details** (bride-groom): notch-lapel gray suit, red bowtie, red
  boutonnière; **strapless/sweetheart white gown** with soft gray fold-shading, a long **sheer
  veil** (very light gray, semi-transparent) and a **train pooling** on the floor.

## 7. Motion
- Small **paired, tapered "swoosh" curves** placed right next to a moving part — a kicking foot,
  a swinging hand, a flaring skirt hem, flying hair. A few marks, small, suggestive — not
  speed-lines.
- Movement is the point: flared skirts, mid-step footwork, wind in the hair, raised pointing arms.

## 8. The background "room" system (include a subset, always faint & behind)
All in a single pale gray (`#e8e8e8`), thin **uniform** line, no fill, clearly receding:
- **Potted plant, lower-left** — a snake-plant / tall pointed leaves in a pot drawn with vertical
  texture lines.
- **Framed picture, upper-right** — a rectangle with a simple hill/horizon line, a small sun
  circle, and a little hanging nub at the top.
- **Small wall brackets** — minimal "⊤"-shaped ticks, mid-height on the walls.
- **Floorboards** — a few long horizontal lines across the lower third.
- **Optional checker dance floor** (weddings) — a receding parallelogram of red / black / white /
  gray squares, foreground.
- Generous **white** everywhere else. Figures own the center; props never compete.

## 9. Composition
- Full-body figures, large, centered, feet in the lower third, comfortable headroom.
- Couples connect cleanly — one handhold (swing) or a closed frame (first dance) where joined
  hands actually meet and the embracing hand actually lands on the partner.
- Group scenes: hero couple biggest and central; guests ringed around slightly smaller; supporting
  elements (DJ) smaller and further back for depth.

## 10. Mood
Joyful, warm, wholesome, a little retro. Country-western socials and weddings. Confident and
inviting — never edgy, never corporate-generic.

## 11. How I (Claude / Claude cowork) actually make these — SVG → render → rasterize

The in-house pipeline — everything happens with Claude in-session (SVG + headless Edge + Pillow).
The critical unlock is the **render-and-look feedback loop** in step 2 — never ship a figure you
haven't rendered and eyeballed (and, for people, shown to Russell — see §11c C).

**Step 1 — Draw each figure in its OWN small SVG** (viewBox ~440×800, one person), to the §11b
armature. Build the body from **filled, tapered shapes** with a uniform ~4px black stroke + round
joins — NOT uniform-width stick strokes (thin sticks + a wide box torso is exactly what read as
"shit"). Part recipes that worked:
- **Torso:** a narrow shape tapering shoulder→waist. Modest shoulders, never a wide box.
- **Trousers/legs:** ONE filled unit with a small crotch notch — fuller thighs, legs close
  together. (Two thin sticks under a wide torso = the failure mode.)
- **Skirt:** one flared filled shape waist→above-knee, slightly wavy hem; bare lower legs + heels below.
- **Arms:** a smooth tapering tube rooted LOW on the shoulder, overlapping the torso. A reaching
  arm is ONE smooth diagonal to the hand — do NOT start with a flat horizontal upper-arm bar
  (that makes a boxy robot shoulder; it took several fixes to learn this).
- **Neck:** short (~30px); raise the bodice/collar so the neck doesn't look elongated.
- **Head/face:** the §11b face kit, ¾-profile toward the partner, **freckles mandatory**; women
  get 2–3 lash flicks + a red lip.

**Step 2 — Render + inspect with headless Edge, fix, repeat:**
```
"/c/Program Files (x86)/Microsoft/Edge/Application/msedge.exe" --headless=new --disable-gpu \
  --hide-scrollbars --force-device-scale-factor=2 --screenshot="fig.png" \
  --window-size=440,800 "file:///ABSOLUTE/PATH/fig.svg"
```
Read `fig.png`, correct the paths, re-render, iterate until on-model.
(cairosvg / reportlab FAIL here — no libcairo. `convert` is Windows' disk tool, NOT ImageMagick.
Pillow 12 + headless Edge is the working combo.)

**Step 3 — Composite the scene with Pillow** onto a white canvas:
- Render each finished figure, trim to its non-white content bbox.
- Scale BOTH figures by the **same factor** (equal head size); align feet to one baseline.
- Man left / woman right; **butt the reaching-hand edges together** (~34px overlap) so hands
  connect (each figure's extreme horizontal point IS its reaching hand).
- Composite each with `PIL.ImageChops.darker` (white merges, black outlines overlap cleanly).
- Add faint floorboard lines, light-gray floor-shadow ellipses, and the signature props
  (potted plant lower-left, framed picture upper-right — crop faint from a source PNG).
- Covers: title in Georgia-Bold, ink `#2f2f41` + red `#e5261f`, with a red rule.

**Step 4 — Save** at target size: web `1200×900`, Facebook event `1920×1005` (1.91:1).

⚠️ **Reality check (2026-07-07):** the *faces/people* are the hard part — flat front faces AND
traced near-profile faces have both been rejected ("faces are messed up"), and cropping the real
figures was rejected too. So don't silently ship SVG people as "done." **Work with Russell
iteratively** (see §11c C): draw one figure, render it, show it beside the reference, take his
specific corrections, repeat — and set the expectation that faces may need several passes. Icons,
props, covers, titles, compositing and copy are solid and low-risk.

## 11b. Character construction spec — measurable (so new characters are ALWAYS consistent)

Draw every person to these fixed rules. Units are **head-heights (HH)**; 1 HH = the height of
the character's own head. This is what keeps a *new* character on-model with the existing set.

**Body armature**
- Total height **7.5 HH**. (Never chibi, never fashion-9-heads.)
- Head 1 HH tall × 0.72 HH wide; soft egg shape, jaw a touch narrower than the cranium.
- Shoulder line at 1.4 HH from crown. Shoulder width: men ~2.0 HH, women ~1.6 HH.
- Waist at 3.0 HH, hips at 3.7 HH. Legs (hip→floor) ~3.8 HH; knee ~5.4 HH from crown.
- Arm: elbow reaches the waist, wrist reaches the hip; hand ≈ 0.85 × head width. Draw real
  fingers in a soft mitten group — never a lump.

**Face — MEASURED ratios from `bride-groom.png` (this is the fix; use these numbers exactly)**
Measured off the groom & bride faces with a pixel grid. Let head height **H** = crown → chin.
Place features at these fractions of H, from the crown down:
- **Eye line: 0.50** (right at the middle — NOT high). Big forehead+cranium above.
- **Nose: 0.62** — and it is **SMALL**: a tiny bump/short line + a nostril dot. (My old faces died
  from a huge pointy nose. Keep it subtle.)
- **Mouth: 0.80** — a **wide, warm smile**, upturned.
- **Chin: 1.00** — so the whole lower face (mouth→chin) is only ~20% of H and is **short and
  ROUNDED**. (My old faces died from a long pointy chin — the #2 killer.)
- **Head width ≈ 0.8 × H**: a wide, rounded head — cranium like a ball, jaw tapering to a soft
  chin. Not a long narrow egg.
- **View: a gentle ¾ turn** (both eyes visible; near eye a touch bigger, far eye smaller and set
  further back with a clear gap). This reads friendlier than a hard profile — match the *groom*.
- **Eyes:** tall almonds with a big dark-dot iris; one **bold** curved brow ~0.06H above each.
  Women add 2–3 lash flicks + a subtle red lip.
- **Hair:** a big glossy mass drawn ON TOP of the cranium (hairline a smooth arc across the
  forehead — no center notch), with real height/quiff. Man: black quiff + sideburn. Woman lead:
  voluminous **red**.
- **Freckles: 3–5 dots on the cheek — MANDATORY.** Small ear tucked at the hairline.
- **Method:** crop the target face from `bride-groom.png`/`country-swing.png`, render your SVG head
  with headless Edge at the same size, paste side-by-side, and adjust to the ratios above. Working
  reference head: scratchpad `head_ratio3.svg` (the approved ¾ face).
- **Top 3 mistakes that make it "garbage":** (1) eyes too high/small; (2) nose too big/pointy;
  (3) chin/lower-face too long & pointy. Fix those three and it snaps into looking right.

**Why these numbers are right — grounding (deep-research, Loomis method, 21/25 claims verified):**
The measured ratios match the classical **Loomis / rule-of-thirds** canon, which is the actual
system behind this clean-line look (the named kits — Open Peeps, Humaaans, Icons8 Ouch! — publish
NO proportion method; they're mix-and-match assets, so the rules come from Loomis).
- Build the head as a **flattened sphere** (cranium = ball, sides sliced flat) with a jaw hung
  below; front-view width ≈ **⅔–0.8 of height** (our refs are ~0.8).
- **Eye line = the head's vertical midline.** "Eyes placed too high" is literally the #1 named
  amateur mistake — which is exactly what wrecked my early faces.
- **Eyes one eye-width apart, equal size** (a third eye fits between). In a ¾ the far eye
  foreshortens narrower and sits closer to the edge — don't shrink both.
- **Equal vertical spacing:** brow→nose = nose→chin; mouth a touch *above* the nose-chin midpoint.
- **¾ turn:** wrap the center line + eye line around the ball and keep the far-side ellipse
  balanced so features don't go lopsided.
- ⚠️ **Refuted schemes to AVOID:** "three equal head-thirds" and "nose halfway / mouth halfway"
  — these were killed 0-3 in verification; use the midline-eye + equal-spacing rules above instead.

**Hair (solid fills)**
- Men: **black**, glossy, with a forelock swoop over the forehead + a defined sideburn; big enough
  to read as a hair mass, not a cap.
- Women: **red `#e5261f`** for the hero/lead (matches the country-swing woman); black for background
  guests. Big voluminous crown + a wavy tail flowing down the back + a wisp framing the face.

**Wardrobe = where the single red accent lives**
- Men: grayscale only (gray suit, or white/light shirt with sleeves optionally rolled + gray
  trousers), black belt + black shoes. One red item max — a tie or bowtie. Skin is white.
- Women: the **red dress** is the accent (knee-length, movement in the skirt), black heels or
  black cowboy boots. Skin is white.

**Line & shading per figure**
- Outer contour line heavy; interior folds/features light; ends taper. Weight ratio ~2:1.
- One soft light-gray tone dropped only into fold pockets; red-dress folds go slightly darker red.
- Every figure gets a soft light-gray floor-shadow ellipse; feet grounded with believable weight.
- 2–3 small tapered motion swooshes beside the moving limb / swinging skirt.

**Consistency test:** two characters drawn to this spec, side by side, must look like they were
drawn by the same hand on the same day as `country-swing.png` / `dance-party.png`.

## 12. Pre-ship checklist
1. Exactly one saturated color, and it's `#e5261f` (dresses / lead's hair / tie / bowtie / checker)?
2. Skin left white; men black-haired & grayscale; guest women black-haired in red dresses?
3. Line has taper/weight variation; soft gray fold-shading; a soft gray floor shadow present?
4. ¾-profile faces with the small-feature kit **and freckles**?
5. Natural ~7.5-head proportions; hands articulated; real footwear; feet grounded?
6. ≥2 faint gray room props behind (plant / framed picture / brackets / floorboards)?
7. Motion swooshes on the moving parts?
8. Sit it beside the three PNGs — does it look like a fourth in the set?
